　　 Richard is the elder brother of two brothers, all scattered around the world. The two men parted ways long before their children were born for a stupid reason neither of them could remember. As it goes, though, they were prideful enough to stick to their former selves' statements and keep the distance.

　　 However, when he found out about the curse that linked all Valentine to Hell due to an old deal their ancestor had made with Lucifer to keep their bloodline going, he'd decided to act. Thus, not long after the birth of his youngest daughter, Violet, he decided to leave to find his brother and find a way out of the loop with him. Stronger together, those were the Valentine words after all.

　　 Unfortunately, Richard never reached his brother and instead, was found by one of Lucifer's demon. The Devil having realized what he was trying to do, 𝘁𝗵𝗲𝘆 interrupted him before he could find any solution to detatching himself and his family from 𝘁𝗵𝗲𝗺. Ever since, he has been stuck in Hell. A breathing, living man emprisoned in a golden cage. Leaving his daughters unaware of their powers, and more importantly, thinking he'd abandoned them.

　　 He remained in Hell for nineteen years until his eldest daughter, Iris, joined him. She'd died protecting her sister. As any Valentine would do. Even without his presence, his girls had stuck together. Stronger together, something they had learned on their own. It was then he also learned his wife, highschool sweetheart, had fallen to her demons and turned to alcohol before meeting an untimely death. By his daughter's hand. Despite the heartbreak that'd caused him, both for his lover and for the daughters he hadn't been around to protect from her, he didn't blame Iris. Magic was unpredictabe, impulsive. Especially to a young, unexperienced with. Though he could tell, through the years, she'd grown to become much more powerful than he's ever been.

　　 As had Violet. Whom he managed to contact through her abilities to see the dead. This was an ability he'd always known she would grow to develop and thus, for two decades, he'd tried to contact her. Until, eventually, he did. It was the first time he'd felt peace in two decades. Warmth. The kind that didn't hurt. He'd almost forgotten how that felt.

　　 A year later, he was brought back among the living. Though he knew, better than anyone, that Lucifer wouldn't simply let it slide.
